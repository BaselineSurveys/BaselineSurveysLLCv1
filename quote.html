About Land Surveying in Colorado

Surveying in Colorado often includes additional considerations such as steep or remote terrain, elevation differences, and seasonal access (snow). These factors can affect the time and cost of a survey. Below are ballpark ranges to help with planning; exact pricing depends on property size, site access, deliverables, and required research.

  • Boundary Survey (residential): $600 – $3,500 — small, accessible lots on the low end; steep, rural, heavily wooded, or complicated boundary histories toward the top end.
  • Topographic Survey: $800 – $4,000 depending on area size and contour detail required.
  • ALTA/NSPS Survey (commercial): $2,000 – $10,000+ depending on parcel size, title/record research, and lender requirements.
  • Construction Staking: $1,000 – $7,000+ depending on project scope and duration.
  • Additional services like as-built mapping are quoted separately.
